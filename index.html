    Vincent van Gogh

    Vincent Willem van Goghwas a Dutch Post-impressionist painter who posthumously became one of the most famous and influential figures in Western art history. In a decade, he created about 2,100 artworks, including around 860 oil paintings, most of which date from the last two years of his life.

    Jackson Pollock

    Paul Jackson Pollock, known as Jackson Pollock, was a famous American painter and a major exponent of vivid abstractionism. He is well known for his special ability of drift painting. During his lifetime, Pollock was famous and infamous. He was a great painter of his generation.

    Johannes Vermeer

    Johannes Vermeer or Jan Vermeer was a Dutch Baroque painter, famous for his works about realistic life. He lived most of his life in the town of Delft. However, Vemeer's life has never been stable, mainly because he only composed a few works.

    Frida Kahlo

    Frida Kahlo de Rivera was a Mexican artist who painted many portraits, self-portraits, and works inspired by nature and Mexican artifacts.

    Kusama Yayoi

    Kusama Yayoi is a Japanese contemporary artist. She has been recognized as one of the most important living artists from Japan, the world's best-selling female artist, and the world's most successful living artist.

    Banksy

    Banksy is the pseudonym of a British graffiti artist, political activist, film director and painter. There is speculation that Swiss artist Maître de Casson may be Banksy. Maître de Casson denies this on its website

    Paul Gauguin

    Eugène Henri Paul Gauguin was a leading painter of the post-impressionist movement. Paul Gauguin was born in Paris, his mother was of white Peruvian descent. When he was young, Paul Gauguin loved adventure and adventure.
